My project is a medieval fantasy setting simulator that tracks various parties of adventurers throughout a large region that reacts to the success and
failure of these inspirational or tragic heroes over the course of 48 years.
This software is projected to successfully cater to an audience of video game enthusiasts that gravitate towards medieval fantasy settings
and simulator mechanics.

Implementation:
std::Maps will be used to track all relevant information for our parties. Keys will be used for storing the name of the groups,
and the values will be std::arrays of 3 lists for each group to encompass [Party members], [Loot], and [Active quests]. 
The initial period at the start of the program sets up a default state of the kingdom in terms of prosperity and safety.
During each period, I'm thinking roughly a month each, I want there to be random events that affect the lists of 
each party; things like party member casualties/ leaving/joining, loot gained and how it affects party strength, 
and Active quests being completed, failed, and new quests acquired.

After each period, the prosperity and safety of the kingdom gets affected by the success and failure of these parties. 

Simulated Events:
Events for each period are random and will include combat event which introduces risks to parties and their members, ranging from flawless victories to
devastating party losses. Other events include quest events which can cause postive or negative modifiers for completion of quests.
Finally, the prosperity and safety variables of the kingdom will be connected to kingdom events that introduces spoils and misfortunes for the parties.
